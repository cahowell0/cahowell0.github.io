Hi.

I'm Christian. I like puzzles and problems. That's why I studied math. Professionally, my title is that of ML Engineer, where ML, of course, stands for Machine Learning. But privately, I like to consider myself more of a Miscel Laneous (ML) Engineer. Where Machine Learning Engineers are expected to use machine learning to solve practical problems, machine learning is just one tool that any engineer can use. There are lots of problems in the world and machine learning may not be the right tool for many of those problems. I want to find solutions to all sorts of problems using all sorts of Miscel Laneous tools (with at least some minimum ethical threshold assumed).

I graduated from Brigham Young University in 2023, majoring in applied math with an emphasis in physics. I also minored in Japanese. Just for fun. I spent some time at Argonne National Lab in Illinois modeling energy usage and regeneration of autonomous electric vehicles. I hopped up to Pennsylvania for a brief stint with the Applied Research Lab at Penn State, working with a team training neural networks to increase the speed and accuracy of TDOA object tracking techniques. Following my flirt with ARL, I accepted a position at MetaSource. My time here is largely spent building programs aimed at increasing the efficiency of reading and classifying document types.

I am always looking for new opportunities, so if you are looking for somebody to join your team to work on difficult and interesting problems, please reach out. I would love to hear from you.
